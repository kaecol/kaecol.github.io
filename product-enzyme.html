Enzymatic Conjugation

What is Enzymatic Conjugation?

Enzymatic conjugation is a bioconjugation technique that uses enzymes to catalyze the formation of covalent bonds between biomolecules, such as proteins or peptides. Unlike chemical conjugation, which often requires harsh conditions and can be less specific, enzymatic conjugation provides high specificity and efficiency, targeting particular sequences or functional groups. This method is widely used in drug development, diagnostics, and research for applications requiring precise control over biomolecule modifications.

Readilase: Ever-ready for your Conjugation Needs

Readilase is based on OaAEP1, an asparaginyl endopeptidase isolated from plant Oldenlandia affinis, known for its lightning-fast catalytic speed, an exception amongst naturally occurring ligases. Through meticulous protein engineering and further product development, we have developed an easy-to-use recombinant ligase, designed to be ever-ready for your conjugation needs.

Short recognition sequence for easy incorporation: Readilase specifically recognizes a short C-terminal tripeptide sequence motif Asn-Xaa-Xaa (e.g. Asn-Gly-Leu), to form a covalent link with compounds bearing an N-terminal dipeptide nucleophile sequence Xaa-Leu (e.g. Gly-Leu).

Comparison with other conjugation enzymes: The patented Readilase is easy-to-use, and performs the conjugation without the use of any chemical or environmental activators. With a conjugation speed much faster than the commonly used Sortase A enzyme, Readilase is capable of bringing your conjugation applications to new heights.

Wide variety of applications: As an ultra-fast conjugation enzyme with a short recognition sequence, Readilase possesses a wide number of applications, ranging from the creation of drug conjugates, to the conjugation of protein or peptide drugs on cells for cell therapy. In essence, Readilase can be used in any application that require peptide/protein conjugation.
