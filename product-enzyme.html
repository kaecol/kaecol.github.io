Enzymatic Conjugation

What is Enzymatic Conjugation?

Enzymatic conjugation is a method that uses special proteins called enzymes to connect biomolecules, such as proteins or peptides. This approach is highly precise and effective, making it ideal for applications in drug development, diagnostics, and research where exact modifications are needed.

Readilase: Ever-ready for your Conjugation Needs

Readilase is an advanced enzyme derived from the plant Oldenlandia affinis. Readilase works exceptionally fast, and performs much faster than other similar enzymes, such as Sortase A. Whether you’re creating drug conjugates or attaching proteins to cells, Readilase provides swift results and reduction in drug manufacturing time.

We have designed Readilase so that it doesn’t require extra chemicals, activators, or special conditions to function. This enables users of Readilase to reduce cost. Readilase is designed to be ever-ready for your conjugation needs.
