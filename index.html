About Us

We are a research group in the School of Materials Science and Engineering, Nanyang Technological University, led by Prof. Tan Lay Poh.

Our laboratory is dedicated to advancing sustainable and biomedical materials research through innovation, collaboration, and education.

Tailor sustainable materials and processes for food and agriculture

We strive to create new materials and processes that minimize environmental impact while delivering high performance and value. We are currently focused on promoting sustainable agriculture and food systems

Lessen or repurpose biowaste via valorization technologies

Our lab is developing innovative waste valorization technologies that enable the transformation of waste into valuable resources. We aim to reduce the environmental impact of waste disposal and promote a circular economy by developing new materials and processes that extract value from waste streams.

Progress the field of biomedical materials

We are keen in discovering novel biomedical materials and technologies that can improve human health and well-being. Our mission is to create safe and effective biomedical materials that meet the highest standards of biocompatibility, functionality, and sustainability.

Our lab specializes in the field of biomaterials engineering and has a range of advanced equipment and resources to support research in this area. Our technical expertise is extensive, with a team of experienced researchers and staff who have advanced degrees in materials science and engineering.

Our Research

Our sustainability and biomedical technology advancements are centered around four major research topics, namely cultivated meat, tissue engineering, waste valorization, and polymer/composite research.

Cultivated Meat

Our lab is developing innovative techniques to create sustainable meat products without animal slaughter. We focus on optimizing cell growth and tissue engineering to produce high-quality meat.

Tissue Engineering

We develop advanced biomaterials and scaffolds to engineer functional and viable living tissues. We also explore the use of stem cells to create personalized and regenerative therapies.

Waste Valorization

Our lab advances the circular economy through waste valorization, converting waste into valuable products. We reduce biowaste by creating sustainable value from waste streams.

Polymers/Composites

We excel in polymer and composite research, creating and engineering advanced materials. These composites have enhanced or unique properties suited for various applications.

Cultivated Meat

Versatile and Cost-Efficient Cultured Meat Platform

Due to growing population and demands, global meat consumption is on the rise. However, the current meat production system is one of the major drivers of environmental degradation. Animal agriculture contributes 14.5% of global greenhouse emissions and occupies 77% of earth’s habitable land while only providing 17% of global calorie supply. The production of animal-based foods is considered an inefficient system as 97% of the calories used for feeding the animals are lost in the entire process. The water consumption for livestock and associated feed crop production also severely reduces global fresh water; 29% of the total water footprint of the agriculture globally is used in the production of animal-based products; out of which one-third of the global water footprint is related to beef cattle. The inefficiencies and unsustainability of animal farming are amplified in Singapore, where resources such as land and water are extremely scarce. Hence the current way of providing meat to feed the population is unsustainable. Cultured meat is a promising alternative to supplement the traditional methods of meat production. One of the main challenges of cultured meat is the cost involved due to the highly complex and expensive culture media.

Our technology focuses on using materials and material designs to circumvent the heavy reliance of expensive and specific culture media for cellular behaviour such as proliferation and differentiation. Using our technology, the process of cell culture is highly simplified, and cost is reduced significantly. The technology is also versatile for producing prototypes of muscle or fat cells with just minimal material designs. Our processing methods and material designs developed are highly scalable.
Waste Valorization

Side stream to high value functional food

The amount of food waste generated have been increasing over the years, with a high percentage of food waste being disposed without recycling. This places pressure on resources to build waste disposal facilities such as waste-to-energy plants and landfills, which is unfavorable for land-scarce countries like Singapore. In addition, decomposing food waste landfills contribute to gaseous emissions which results in global warming. Often, food waste are excellent sources of value-added products as they have high organic content. For instance, food waste such as fish skin and bullfrog skin are rich in collagen which can be extracted. Collagen is often used in tissue engineering and wound healing applications, while its derivative, gelatin, is often used in food applications as an additive for baking, snacks and candies. These products are rich in protein, and have unique amino acid profiles that gives it many potential health benefits.

In our laboratory we investigate and manipulate the structure-property relationship of the extracted collagen and gelatin to obtain materials with a range of mechanical, thermal, and textural properties for use in a wide range of food products. These final products have high amino acid contents and are good source of protein nutrition. Their properties can be tailored for human or animal consumption.
