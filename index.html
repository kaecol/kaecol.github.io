Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs)

If you are a pharmaceutical company developing ADCs, your aim is to deliver cancer drugs directly to tumors, minimizing harm to healthy cells and improving patient outcomes.

The biggest challenge is ensuring the drug is attached to the antibody in a precise and controlled way. If the drug randomly attaches to the wrong part of the antibody, it can reduce the antibody’s ability to find and bind to cancer cells, leading to off-target effects and reduced therapeutic effectiveness.

Our Azide Maleimide Kit ensures that drugs are attached to specific hinges on the antibody, preserving its targeting function. This precise conjugation helps you develop more effective ADCs with better controlled drug antibody ratio (DAR) for effective therapy.

Lipid Nanoparticles (LNPs)

If you are a drug delivery biotech using LNPs, precise delivery of therapeutic payloads such as mRNA and gene-editing tools (e.g., CRISPR) is crucial for treatments like gene therapy.

The major challenge lies in ensuring that LNPs reach the right target cells. Without proper targeting, these nanoparticles may deliver their mRNA or gene-editing tools to unintended tissues, reducing the effectiveness of the treatment and potentially causing off-target effects. In gene editing, this can lead to unintended modifications in healthy cells, which can be harmful.

Our DBCO NHS Ester Kit allows you to functionalize LNPs by attaching targeting molecules like antibodies or peptides, guiding them to specific cells or tissues. This targeted delivery ensures that gene-editing tools and therapeutic payloads are delivered precisely where they’re needed, enhancing the safety and efficacy of gene therapies and reducing off-target effects.

Extracellular Vesicles (EVs)

If you are a regenerative medicine research group using EVs to promote tissue repair, understanding where and how these vesicles function in the body is key to optimizing their therapeutic potential.

The challenge is that most methods of labeling EVs can interfere with their natural function, complicating research and hindering their therapeutic use. If researchers can’t track the EVs without altering their properties, it becomes difficult to assess their true regenerative capabilities.

Our Azide PTAD Kit provides a way to label EVs selectively, targeting a rare moiety without disrupting the functional proteins. This allows you to track and study EVs in vivo, ensuring observation of how the vesicles work while preserving their regenerative function.

Cell-Drug Conjugates

If you are a cell therapy company engineering immune cells/red blood cells to treat cancer, improving the persistence and effectiveness of these cells in targeting tumors is crucial for treatment success.

A key issue is that immune cells often don’t last long enough in the body or penetrate tumors effectively. Adding helper or armouring proteins to cells is necessary to enable that functionality, but this modification process must be done carefully, or it can damage the cells and weaken their therapeutic abilities.

Our Readilase Conjugation Kit offers a gentle method for attaching proteins to immune cells, boosting their circulation and tumor infiltration capabilities without harming their function. This allows you to create more resilient and effective immune cells for cancer immunotherapy.
